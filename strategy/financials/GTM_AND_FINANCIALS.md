## 1. Executive Summary

WTH Drive Rentals enters the South African mobility market as an infrastructure layer — converting idle vehicle inventory into accessible, flexible rentals. This document outlines the go-to-market strategy across three phases (MVP → Dealer Exchange → VaaS) with detailed financial projections, unit economics, and market segmentation.

**3-Year Financial Target:** ZAR 126M GMV by Year 3, with ZAR 23.5M platform revenue at 18.6% take rate.

## 3. GTM Phases

### Phase 1: MVP Launch (Months 1-6) — P2P Marketplace

**Objective:** Prove demand-supply matching in Sandton/Cape Town CBD with 200 vehicles listed, 500 active renters.

**Launch Date:** Month 3 (after 3-month build)

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Product** | Mobile app (iOS + Android) for renters; web dashboard for owners; basic Trust Score with KYC + financial inputs |
| **Supply Acquisition** | Onboarding campaigns targeting private car owners in gated estates, office parks, university precincts |
| **Demand Acquisition** | Gig driver partnerships (Uber driver lounges); university student WhatsApp groups; corporate HR commuter programs |
| **Marketing Channels** | Facebook/Instagram lead gen, Google Ads (local service ads), referral program (ZAR 500 per referral) |
| **Revenue Model** | 25% commission on P2P bookings |
| **Risk Product** | WDR Shield at ZAR 99-199 per booking (deposit waiver) |
| **KPI Targets** | 200 active listings, 500 bookings/month, 60% utilization on listed fleet |
| **Cash Burn** | ~ZAR 1.5M (app dev, marketing, ops team of 5) |

**Key Hires:** Product Manager, Community Manager (Supply-side), Growth Marketer, Operations Coordinator, Compliance Officer

---

### Phase 2: Dealer Exchange (Months 7-12)

**Objective:** Onboard 50 dealerships across JHB/CPT, adding 500+ premium inventory units.

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Product** | Dealer dashboard for inventory management; integrated Trust Score v2 (+behavioral); trip-based telematics overlay (OBD-II dongle program) |
| **Dealer Acquisition** | Target demo/used car managers at franchised dealers (Barloworld, McCarthy, Group1, Motus); value prop: "Monetise parked stock" |

| **Marketing Channels** | Trade events (NADA show), direct B2B sales team (2 dealers/month target per rep), automotive digital ads |
| **Insurance** | Partner with Hollard/Old Mutual for trip-based overlay; dealer stock covered under existing dealer insurance + WDR gap |
| **KPI Targets** | 50 dealerships onboarded, 1,000 total listings, 2,000 bookings/month, 30% fleet utilization |
| **Cash Burn** | ~ZAR 3M cumulative (BD team expansion, telematics hardware, marketing, ops scaling) |

**Key Hires:** 2x Business Development Managers (Dealer channels), 2x Account Managers, Insurance Partnerships Lead

### Phase 3: VaaS (Vehicle-as-a-Service) & Scale (Months 13-36)

**Objective:** Launch subscription products for gig workers and SMEs; expand to Durban/Pretoria; reach 5,000+ units.

| Component | Detail |

| **KPI Targets** | 3,000 active subscribers, 5,000 total listings, 5,000+ bookings/month, 50% fleet utilization, ZAR 2M+ MRR |
| **Cash Burn** | ~ZAR 8M cumulative (tech platform maturity, ops scaling to 20+ staff, telematics infrastructure) |

**Key Hires:** Head of Fleet Operations, 5x B2B Sales Reps, Customer Success team (4), Data Scientist (Trust Score model refinement)

## 4. Unit Economics

### 4.1 Assumptions

| Parameter | Value | Source / Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Average Booking Value (P2P) | ZAR 850/day × 3 days = ZAR 2,550 | SA rental market benchmark (Avis/eNTL) |
| Average Booking Value (Dealer) | ZAR 650/day × 4 days = ZAR 2,600 | Slightly lower daily rate, longer duration |
| Average Booking Value (VaaS) | ZAR 4,800/month | Blended across Gig/SME/Premium tiers |
| Commission Rate (P2P) | 25% | Competitive marketplace standard |
| Commission Rate (Dealer) | 20% | Volume discount for dealers |
| WDR Shield Fee | ZAR 129/booking | 5% of booking value (PT Barnum approach) |
| WDR Plus Membership | ZAR 299/month | Estimated at 1-2% of renter monthly spend |
| VaaS Gross Margin | 18% | Fleet costs (lease/insurance/telematics) |
| Gross Merchandise Value (GMV) | Total transaction volume | Sum of all bookings/subscriptions |

### 4.2 Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

| Channel | CAC (Year 1) | CAC (Year 3) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Digital Ads (FB/Google) | ZAR 450 | ZAR 350 | Scale efficiency as brand grows |
| Referral Program | ZAR 500/booking | ZAR 500/booking | Fixed incentive per referred booking |
| Dealer B2B (per dealer) | ZAR 5,000 | ZAR 3,000 | Sales rep time + demo costs |
| VaaS B2B (per SME contract) | ZAR 8,000 | ZAR 5,000 | Contract value covers high CAC |
| Organic / Brand | ZAR 0 | ZAR 50 | Brand awareness reduces paid needs |
| **Blended CAC (Renters)** | **ZAR 280** | **ZAR 180** | Weighted average across channels |

### 4.3 Lifetime Value (LTV)

| Segment | Avg. Monthly Spend | Retention (Monthly) | Avg. Lifetime (Months) | Gross LTV | Net LTV (After COGS) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Casual Renter (P2P) | ZAR 1,275 | 20% | 2.5 (total, not consecutive) | ZAR 3,188 | ZAR 2,391 |
| Regular Renter (Dealer/P2P) | ZAR 2,550 | 30% | 6 months active | ZAR 15,300 | ZAR 11,475 |
| Gig Worker (VaaS) | ZAR 10,000 | 45% | 8 months | ZAR 80,000 | ZAR 14,400 (18% margin) |
| SME Fleet (VaaS) | ZAR 16,000 | 55% | 12 months | ZAR 192,000 | ZAR 34,560 (18% margin) |

**LTV:CAC Ratio:**
- Casual Renter: 8.5:1 ✅
- Regular Renter: 41:1 ✅✅
- Gig Worker: **50:1** ✅✅✅ (Note: VC-backed gig businesses often subsidize this)
- SME Fleet: **69:1** ✅✅✅

> **Target LTV:CAC > 3:1** for cash-efficient growth. WDR achieves this easily for repeat segments but must focus on converting casual → regular renters.

## 7. Key Assumptions & Risks

### Assumptions
1. SA digital payment adoption continues at 15% CAGR (Payfast/Yoco/Ozow data)
2. No major regulatory changes (NCR reclassification, new mobility tax)
3. Vehicle theft/damage rates remain within insurance modeling parameters (Trust Alpha reduces default by 30%+)
4. SA ZAR holds relatively stable against USD (for hardware/API costs)
5. Fuel price stability (affects gig worker economics but rental demand is inelastic)

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Deposit waiver underpriced** | Underwriting losses | Medium | Dynamic pricing via Trust Score; reinsurance; monthly pricing review |
| **Supply-side churn** | Low inventory, reduced marketplace | Medium | Owner incentives (referral, free telematics); automated vehicle management |
| **Demand-side fraud** | Stolen vehicles, chargebacks | Medium | Biometric KYC; GPS immobilization; payment escrow; fraud ML model |
| **Regulatory reclassification** | NCR registration required | Low | Legal opinion upfront; proactive engagement with NCR |
| **Fleet under-utilization** | Inventory glut | Medium | Price optimization engine; demand-side promotions; flexible cancellation |
| **Insurance partner exit** | No coverage for rentals | Low | Multi-insurer agreements; captive pool as fallback |
